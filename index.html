Maintaining your new hair is the key to a long lasting look. Follow this guide below to make sure you get the most out of your investment.

Good habits

    Daily

    -Brush gently. Use your free hand to secure the base of the weft.

    -Use a wet brush or boar bristle brush.

    -Sleep in a low pony or braid. Use a silk pillow or head scarf.

    -Use wet/dry oil (clear only).

    -follow this link for more tips

    Washing

    -Deep condition weekly. Bi-weekly if possible.

    -Brush through the ends before shampooing.

    -Use salon grade sulfate free shampoo/conditioner.

    -Shampooing in the shower is best/easiest. RINSE, RINSE, RINSE.

    -No conditioner at top of the weft (can cause slipping).

    -follow this link for more tips

Bad habits

    Never

    -Don't be rough while brushing or any other activity.

    -Always avoid chlorinated/salt water.

    -Use salon grade sulfate free shampoo/conditioner.

    -Avoid chlorinated/salt water.

    -No sunscreens, tanning oils/lotions on extensions (esp. lighter hair).

    -Avoid over use of purple/blue shampoos.

    -Do not go to sleep with wet hair. Always keep dry.

    -No fast drying sprays and dry shampoo.

    -Avoid protein fill products.

    -Avoid spraying hair spray or dry texture spray before heat styling.

    -Do not scrub w/ nails on weft rows, use the pads of finger tips.

    -NEVER HAVE BLEACH APPLIED TO YOUR EXTENSIONS.

Extensions

Bead Extensions are the safest method for weft extensions on the market, and my preferred method. Style your hair anyway you want including having a hidden ponytail. The key to this method is having both a tension and irritation free application.

I.B.E. consists of applying beads to your section of hair, then applying hand tied wefts above and below to create a completely seamless application. You can add volume, length, or both to your hair and almost everyone is a candidate.

I.B.E. does require maintenance of 7-12 weeks depending on the person. We do not "tighten" or "move up" the wefts as most methods do, but rather re-install them fresh. I suggest clients repurchase individual wefts after a few months/re-installs. This will help from needing to buy an entire new set of hair.
